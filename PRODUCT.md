# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Med and law students who are serious, daily Anki users. They process large volumes of dense PDFs — lecture slides, case law, textbook chapters — and need to turn them into flashcard decks fast. They open this app with a specific document in hand and a specific exam on the horizon. They know what Anki is; they don't need hand-holding. Context: laptop, focused study session, often under time pressure.

## Product Purpose

Converts uploaded PDFs or pasted text into Anki-ready `.apkg` flashcard decks using Google Gemini AI. Users control card density (High-Yield, Comprehensive, Granular) and card style (Standard, Cloze, MCQ, etc.). A Settings Recommender panel helps optimize Anki scheduling parameters for their specific deck size, exam timeline, and daily time budget. Success: deck is downloaded and imported into Anki in under two minutes, with settings already dialed in.

## Brand Personality

Confident, precise, premium. Stripe/Vercel energy: typographically strong, polished, elevated without ornamentation. The amber accent palette grounds it in study warmth without going cozy or academic-nostalgic. Voice is terse and expert — no cheerful microcopy, no hand-holding.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS cream: off-white cards everywhere, gradient CTAs, rounded-everything, hero metrics, decorative illustrations
- Dark neon / hacker aesthetic: terminal greens, glowing borders, crypto-tool look
- Clinical / sterile: pure white and grey with zero warmth — feels like a government form
- Overly playful / bubbly: emoji-heavy, bouncy animations, consumer-app cheerfulness

## Design Principles

1. **Expert confidence.** The UI assumes the user knows Anki. No unnecessary explainers, no wizard flows, no "getting started" fluff.
2. **Precision over decoration.** Every visual element serves a function. Amber is not brand color — it's signal: active state, progress, success. Slate is structure. Nothing is decorative.
3. **Density with breathing room.** Med students work with dense information. The layout handles it without claustrophobia: tight controls, generous whitespace around the drop target.
4. **Terse and direct.** Labels are short. States communicate with minimum words. No sentences where a word will do.
5. **Premium by restraint.** Quality shows through typography, spacing precision, and consistent token use — not gradients, shadows, or added components.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Keyboard navigable. Focus rings on all interactive elements. Color is never the sole signal for state (border + background change together on active states).
